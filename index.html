GPUs & CPUs

What is a GPU?

The GPU, or graphics processing unit, is a specialized electronic circuit initially designed to accelerate computer graphics and image processing. In a desktop or laptop GPUs come mainly in two forms, video cards or integrated graphics. Video cards, also known as graphics cards, are computer expansion cards that generates a feed of graphics output to a display device, usually a monitor. Integrated graphics come in two forms, iGPUs (Intel) and APUs (AMD). Integrated grapics is a term used for processors (CPUs) that have a GPU built into them.

What is a CPU?

The CPU, or central processing unit, executes instructions of a computer program, such as arithmetic, logic, controlling, and input/output (I/O). In short it is like the brain of your desktop or laptop.
